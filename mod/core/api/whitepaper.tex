\begin{abstract}
We present Prefi, a decentralized prediction market protocol that enables participants to wager on future asset prices through a novel epoch-based competition mechanism. Like Einstein's revolutionary insights into the nature of space-time through thought experiments, we explore the fundamental principles governing collective price discovery and the distribution of rewards based on predictive accuracy. This system employs L1 distance metrics for scoring, modular oracle architecture for price aggregation, and zero-sum game theory for fair reward distribution.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction: The Thought Experiment}

Imagine, if you will, a marketplace where the collective wisdom of participants converges toward truth—not through authority, but through the elegant mathematics of accuracy and stake. Einstein taught us that the universe operates on principles of relativity and invariance; similarly, our prediction market operates on the invariant principle that \textit{accuracy weighted by conviction} determines value.

The fundamental question we address: \textbf{How can we create a fair, transparent, and mathematically rigorous system for rewarding predictive accuracy in decentralized markets?}

\section{The Fundamental Principles}

\subsection{Principle of Weighted Conviction}
Just as Einstein's $E=mc^2$ relates energy to mass, our system relates reward to the product of accuracy and stake:

\begin{equation}
\text{Score} = \frac{\text{LockedAmount}}{1 + |\text{Predicted} - \text{Actual}|}
\end{equation}

This elegant formula embodies two truths:
\begin{itemize}
\item Greater stake (conviction) amplifies reward potential
\item Greater accuracy (lower distance) increases score exponentially
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Principle of Zero-Sum Conservation}
Like conservation of energy in physics, our system conserves value:

\begin{equation}
\sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{Reward}_i = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{LockedAmount}_i
\end{equation}

No value is created or destroyed—only redistributed based on merit.

\subsection{Principle of Temporal Epochs}
Time is divided into discrete epochs, each a self-contained universe of predictions:

\begin{equation}
\text{Epoch} = [t_{\text{start}}, t_{\text{end}}] \rightarrow \text{Price}_{\text{actual}}
\end{equation}

\section{The Mathematical Framework}

\subsection{L1 Distance Metric}
We employ the Manhattan distance (L1 norm) for measuring prediction accuracy:

\begin{equation}
d_{L1}(p, a) = |p - a|
\end{equation}

where $p$ is predicted price and $a$ is actual price.

\textbf{Why L1?} Like choosing the simplest coordinate system in relativity, L1 distance provides:
\begin{itemize}
\item Computational simplicity
\item Linear penalty for deviation
\item Intuitive interpretation (absolute error)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Score Calculation}
For player $i$ with prediction $p_i$ and locked amount $\ell_i$:

\begin{equation}
s_i = \frac{\ell_i}{1 + d_{L1}(p_i, a)} = \frac{\ell_i}{1 + |p_i - a|}
\end{equation}

The denominator's $+1$ prevents division by zero and ensures finite scores.

\subsection{Reward Distribution}
The reward for player $i$ from total pool $P$:

\begin{equation}
R_i = \frac{s_i}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} s_j} \times P
\end{equation}

This is a \textit{proportional distribution} based on relative merit.

\section{Example: A Thought Experiment}

Consider three observers predicting Bitcoin's price at epoch end:

\textbf{Actual Price:} \$100,000

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
\textbf{Player} & \textbf{Predicted} & \textbf{Locked} & \textbf{Distance} & \textbf{Score} & \textbf{Reward} \\
\hline
Alice & \$102,000 & 1000 & 2000 & 0.4998 & 918 \\
Bob & \$95,000 & 500 & 5000 & 0.0833 & 230 \\
Carol & \$100,000 & 200 & 0 & 200 & 552 \\
\hline
\textbf{Total} & - & \textbf{1700} & - & \textbf{200.583} & \textbf{1700} \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Observations:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Carol's perfect prediction yields highest score per token
\item Alice's large stake compensates for small error
\item Bob's large error diminishes returns despite stake
\item Total rewards equal total locked (conservation)
\end{itemize}

\section{The Oracle System: Aggregating Truth}

Like Einstein's reliance on multiple reference frames, we aggregate prices from multiple oracles:

\subsection{Modular Architecture}
\begin{equation}
\text{Price}_{\text{aggregated}} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{Price}_i^{\text{oracle}}
\end{equation}

Supported oracles:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Chainlink}: Decentralized, high reliability
\item \textbf{Pyth}: High-frequency, low latency
\item \textbf{Uniswap V3}: DEX-based TWAP
\item \textbf{Binance}: CEX price feeds
\item \textbf{CoinGecko}: Aggregated market data
\item \textbf{CoinMarketCap}: Market cap weighted
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Confidence Scoring}
Confidence based on maximum deviation:

\begin{equation}
\text{Confidence} = 1 - \frac{\max_i |\text{Price}_i - \text{Price}_{\text{avg}}|}{\text{Price}_{\text{avg}}}
\end{equation}
